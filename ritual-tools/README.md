# Ritual Tools
**Structured ceremonies for team alignment and decision-making**

## Purpose
These apps provide structure for important team rituals ‚Äî the moments that need more than casual conversation but less than heavy process. Use them for sprint kickoffs, decision tracking, alignment verification, and project context documentation.

---

## Apps in This Category

### ‚úì [Co-Sign](https://maurakrandall.github.io/ritual-tools/co-sign.html)
**Lightweight commitment tracking for team decisions**

Get clear sign-off line-by-line, with friendly accountability that actually sticks. Perfect for:
- Product roadmap commitments
- Sprint goals and milestones
- Cross-functional agreements
- Avoiding the "crickets problem" where teams give casual nods but later claim they never agreed

**How it works:** 
1. Creator lists commitments line-by-line
2. Team members respond to each: "I'm in" / "Need more info" / "Can't commit"
3. Dashboard shows exactly who's committed, who has questions, and what's blocking people

**Key innovation:** Solves the problem where everyone seems aligned in the moment, but you discover disagreement later. Co-Sign creates specific, trackable commitments.

### ‚öñÔ∏è [AlignFirst](https://maurakrandall.github.io/ritual-tools/align-first.html)
**Verify team alignment before moving forward**

Make sure everyone's on the same page before committing resources. Perfect for:
- Before starting new initiatives
- When assumptions feel shaky
- After strategy shifts
- Preventing misalignment from compounding

**How it works:**
1. State the decision or direction
2. Team rates alignment 1-10
3. Low scorers explain gaps
4. Team addresses concerns before proceeding

**Key innovation:** Catches misalignment BEFORE it becomes expensive. Better to spend 10 minutes aligning now than 10 weeks building the wrong thing.

### üöÄ [Sprint Kickoff](https://maurakrandall.github.io/ritual-tools/sprint-kickoff.html)
**Start strong with clear goals and team alignment**

Transform sprint planning from checkbox exercise to genuine alignment. Perfect for:
- Starting new sprints
- Quarterly planning
- Project kickoffs
- Any time you need clear shared goals

**How it works:**
1. Set sprint goals and success criteria
2. Import team context (optional)
3. Team reviews and commits
4. Dashboard tracks progress

**Key innovation:** Combines goal-setting with commitment-tracking. Everyone knows not just WHAT we're doing, but WHO's doing WHAT and WHY it matters.

### üìö [Project Binder Generator](https://maurakrandall.github.io/ritual-tools/project-binder-generator.html)
**Create comprehensive project context for AI collaboration**

Document everything AI needs to be a useful teammate on your project. Perfect for:
- Starting new AI collaborations
- Onboarding AI to existing projects
- Creating portable context across tools
- Teaching AI your team's voice and values

**How it works:**
1. Answer structured questions about your project
2. Define team roles and communication style
3. Document constraints and success criteria
4. Export as markdown for any AI tool

**Key innovation:** Treats "teaching AI about your project" as a first-class activity, not an afterthought. The binder becomes living documentation that humans AND AI can reference.

## When to Use Ritual Tools

**Sprint Planning:** Sprint Kickoff sets clear goals, Co-Sign gets commitments

**Major Decisions:** AlignFirst verifies understanding, Co-Sign tracks who's in

**New Projects:** Project Binder documents context, Sprint Kickoff launches work

**Quarterly Planning:** AlignFirst on strategy, Co-Sign on initiatives, Sprint Kickoff for execution

## Design Philosophy

All Ritual Tools follow these principles:
- **Ceremony without bureaucracy:** Structure that helps, not hinders
- **Explicit > implicit:** Make assumptions visible
- **Async-first, sync-optional:** Work across time zones
- **Context is everything:** Capture and carry forward what matters
- **Human-AI collaboration:** Design for teams that include AI

---

## The "Crickets Problem"

Many teams experience this pattern:
1. Present an idea in a meeting
2. Everyone nods
3. Later, discover people had concerns but didn't speak up
4. OR people genuinely thought they agreed but had different mental models

**Ritual Tools solve this by:**
- Making commitments explicit and line-by-line
- Creating safe space for questions and concerns
- Documenting WHO committed to WHAT
- Showing gaps in alignment BEFORE they become expensive
