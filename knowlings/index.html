§ 01The Thesis
The world is awash in answers.
What it lacks is knowing.

Search gives you information. LLMs give you synthesis. Neither gives you knowing — the lived, contextual, hard-won sense that your situation is named, that others have been here, that a way through exists. Knowing is social, slow, and cumulative. It is what a platform can build — and what a chatbot, alone, cannot.

KNOWlings is a platform where the act of shedding an unknown — naming it, sitting with it, contributing to its aggregate — is the product. Knowns grow. Unknowns shred. The meter rises. Together.

The Three Gaps
I
Information → Knowing
Search returns links. Nobody has read them with you.
II
Isolation → Belonging
You are not the only person who typed that query today.
III
Advice → Wisdom
Wisdom is attributed, earned, and layered. Not scraped.
§ 02The Know Meter
Five phases. One meter.
Never complete.

A Knowling's relationship to a subject moves through five phases. The Know Meter is never finished — Knowing is not a terminal state but an ongoing one. The meter is shared: each tap, label and Knowing shifts the aggregate for everyone else in the vertical.

Design principle
"Sharing" is not a phase. It runs beneath all phases as a horizontal line — anonymous at the left, attributed at the right. Users can walk the meter without ever sharing. The platform still grows.
§ 03The Loop
Eight steps.
Step zero counts.

Every KNOWlings interaction walks the same eight-step loop. The radical move is Step 0: the search itself is a contribution. Before a user has created an account, written a word, or made a choice, their arrival has already shifted the aggregate. You are not alone is the first feature, not the last.

§ 04Contribution Levels
The lowest conscious contribution is the default.

Most platforms assume posting. KNOWlings assumes tapping. Four escalating levels of contribution each take more from the user and give back more reputation. The contribution spectrum runs parallel to the Know Meter — you can walk the meter at any contribution level.

§ 05The Human-AI Loop
AI is a teammate,
never a teacher.

The platform is a three-participant conversation: the Knowling, their community, and Claude. AI appears at moments of reframe — never at moments of naming. The human names the experience; AI offers a pattern; the human chooses whether to try the reframe or stay with their word.

AI surfaces patterns — across the aggregate — "24 others arrived at this word."
AI offers reframes — never diagnoses. The user picks whether to try them.
AI stays invisible — in the act of writing a Knowing. The voice is the user’s.
AI is attributed — when it speaks. "Claude · teammate, not teacher."
Design contract
AI speaks second. The human names the thing first. The community nods. Only then does Claude offer a reframe — labeled, bounded, and refusable.
Three failure modes to avoid
AI-as-diagnosis — "you are burned out" (prescriptive, extractive)
AI-as-ghostwriter — writing Knowings on the user’s behalf
AI-as-priest — speaking before the human has named their thing
§ 06The Verticals
One loop.
Many verticals.

The platform primitive is the loop. A vertical is a domain of unknowns the loop is tuned to — Markets, Burnout, Leadership, Wine. Each vertical has its own voices, its own aggregate, its own Knowings. Knowlings earn Ace standing inside a vertical by being the person others name-check.

§ 08What Comes Next
A platform is a promise
to the next Knowling.

Someone will arrive tomorrow with the same unknown you had today. The platform's job is to make sure they find what you named — and that the aggregate tells them they are not the first.
