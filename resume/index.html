About

I grew up as a foreign-service kid — five countries before I was thirteen. You learn to read a room you've never been in before, and to find the thread that connects people who don't yet speak the same language. That's been the job ever since: translating between engineers, editors, executives, and users, and getting them to build something together.

The people who've seen me work tend to stick around. Patricia Lawless led engineering on Yahoo! Shine while I led product; James Long was an engineer on her team. Years later I hired James at Condé Nast — and years after that, both of them wrote me recommendations. When I had to choose between a vocal advertiser and an independent blogger's editorial integrity, I chose the blogger, and worked the relationship until it held. That's the version of leadership I keep showing up with.
