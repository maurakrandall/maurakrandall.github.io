Strategic Point of View

One Experience.

1,500 AI agents. Seven platforms. Seventeen thousand employees. The next chapter isn't just connecting platforms — it's the intelligence that becomes possible once they're connected.

The Thesis

Expedia's employees didn't wait for permission. Since January 2025, they've built over 1,500 AI agents. Two-thirds of engineering uses AI coding assistants. Six thousand sessions a month in the AI playground. That's not an adoption problem. That's a coherence problem.

Workday, ServiceNow, Glean, Slack, Zoom, Atlassian, endpoint platforms — each running its own AI experiments. The employee experience isn't one experience. It's seven experiences bolted together by the employee's own patience.
The integration layer is the backbone. But it's not the end state. Once the platforms connect, a new layer becomes possible — AI that reads patterns across systems, surfaces what humans don't have time to see, and shifts the employee experience from reactive to proactive.
The next chapter isn't just connecting platforms.
It's the intelligence that becomes possible once they're connected.

Two Chapters

Chapter One was building. Build AI stuff. Measure what you shipped. Celebrate adoption. When AI was new, the only move was to get it into people's hands and see what stuck. Expedia ran Chapter One exceptionally well.

Chapter Two is composing. The question shifts from "are people using AI?" to "is AI making the employee experience better?" — and then to the question most organizations never reach: "is AI seeing things we can't?"

Chapter One: are people using AI?
Chapter Two: is it working?
Chapter Three: is it seeing what we can't?

The role isn't slowing down innovation. It's aiming it — and then building the intelligence layer that makes the whole greater than the sum of 1,500 parts.

Three Shifts

The Next Chapter Requires

Shift 01
Agent sprawl → Governed ecosystem

1,500 agents is a sign of energy, not strategy. Some solve real problems. Some duplicate each other. Some were experiments that should have ended. The next chapter requires knowing the difference — and building the governance to retire what doesn't serve without killing the experimentation culture that produced what does.

The discipline isn't building less. It's knowing what to keep.
Shift 02
Platform silos → Intelligence layer

Workday knows who the employee is. ServiceNow knows what they need. Glean knows what they're looking for. Slack knows who they talk to. Atlassian knows what they're building. Today, the employee carries context between systems manually. The integration layer removes that burden. The intelligence layer — the proactive agents in every beat above — is what becomes possible once the data flows.

The employee should never be the API. And the API should never be the end state.
Shift 03
Adoption metrics → Outcome metrics

Chapter One measured: agents built, playground sessions, features shipped. Those were the right metrics for an experimentation phase. Chapter Two measures what actually changed.

Not thisMeasure this
Agents builtFriction eliminated
Playground sessionsTime-to-productive for new hires
AI features shippedDecision quality + speed
Tool loginsEmployee satisfaction (ESAT)
Adoption dashboardsProactive interventions that landed

Adoption tells you what people tried. Outcomes tell you what worked. The distance between those two numbers is the coherence gap.

About This Artifact

This is a point of view — not a roadmap. Built from the outside, informed by public data, designed to be pressure-tested by people who know the employee experience from the inside.

The leader who builds this holds two things simultaneously: the technical fluency to architect integrations across seven enterprise platforms — and the organizational fluency to align IT, People Technology, HR, Finance, Security, and Engineering around a single experience vision.

The integration layer is essential. It's the backbone that makes everything else possible. But the real prize is what emerges once the platforms connect: intelligence that sees patterns humans don't have time to see, surfaces opportunities before they're asked for, and shifts the employee experience from something people endure to something that works for them.

Expedia's employees already adopted AI.
The platforms are ready to connect.
Now build the intelligence layer that makes it one experience.
